Six Dimensions of Control Intelligence

Every CRI Diagnostic Statement is evaluated across six operational dimensions.
Each dimension answers a distinct question about how a control works, enforces, and evidences.

01 Steering Layer
Governance Intent
Defines whether a control establishes policy, accountability, or organizational direction. Controls with governance intent require formal decision-making, documented standards, and management oversight to be considered effectively implemented.
02 Shield Layer
Technical Enforcement
Identifies controls enforced through systems, configurations, or technical mechanisms — blocking, restricting, or preventing behavior without relying on human action at the point of execution.
03 Eyes Layer
Monitoring
Captures controls that observe, detect, log, or evaluate system state and behavior — generating the visibility and audit evidence that examiners and regulators require to verify control effectiveness.
04 Speed Layer
Automation
Flags controls where execution is performed automatically by systems — no manual intervention required. Trigger-based, programmatic, and repeatable at scale. Where Automation is present, Technical Enforcement must also exist.
05 Flow Layer
Lifecycle
Recognizes controls that are not one-time actions but ongoing operational processes — provisioning, maintenance, periodic reviews, and continuous management over time. Lifecycle controls demand sustained operational commitment.
06 Direction Layer
Strategic Intent
Reserved for controls that establish high-level direction or existence statements — where no specific governance, technical, monitoring, automation, or lifecycle mechanics are present. A signal of intent without operational execution.
